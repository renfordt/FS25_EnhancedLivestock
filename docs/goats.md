# Goats

Goats are hardy, versatile livestock known for their browsing ability and dairy production.

## Breeds

### Standard Goat
The default goat breed available in the game.

| Property | Female (Doe) | Male (Buck) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Target Weight | 30 kg | 35 kg |
| Max Weight | 90 kg | 115 kg |
| Birth Weight | 3.75 kg | 4.0 kg |

## Reproduction

### Fertility
Goats typically reach breeding age at 12 months.

### Pregnancy
- **Gestation period:** Approximately 5 months
- **Litter size:** 1-3 kids
- **Minimum health for breeding:** 75%

!!! info "Prolific Breeders"
    Goats can breed year-round in good conditions, making them efficient for dairy operations.

## Production

### Milk
Goat milk is a specialty product with different properties than cow milk.

- Used for goat cheese production
- Lactation follows pregnancy cycle
- Production affected by genetics and nutrition

### Manure Output

| Output Type | Amount |
|-------------|--------|
| Manure | Low-Medium |
| Liquid Manure | Low-Medium |

## Input Requirements

Goats are browsers rather than grazers:

- Prefer browse (leaves, twigs, shrubs)
- Require fresh water
- Need mineral supplements

!!! tip "Feeding Goats"
    Goats are less efficient at utilizing grass compared to sheep but excel at clearing brush and weeds.

## Goat Characteristics

### Advantages
- **Hardy** - Adapt to poor conditions
- **Efficient browsers** - Clear brush effectively
- **Year-round breeding** - No strict seasonal cycle
- **Low maintenance** - Require less intensive care
- **Agile** - Can access terrain other livestock cannot

### Challenges
- **Escape artists** - Require secure fencing
- **Destructive browsing** - Will eat valuable plants
- **Lower milk volume** - Produce less than cows
- **Strong flavor** - Goat products have distinct taste

## Management Tips

!!! tip "Successful Goat Farming"
    - **Secure fencing** - Goats are notorious escape artists
    - **Companion animals** - Goats prefer company
    - **Shelter** - Despite hardiness, need protection from extreme weather
    - **Hoof care** - Regular trimming essential
    - **Buck management** - Keep bucks separate except during breeding
