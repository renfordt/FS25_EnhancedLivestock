## Reproduction

### Fertility
Sheep typically reach breeding age at 12 months.

### Pregnancy
- **Gestation period:** Approximately 5 months
- **Litter size:** 1-3 lambs
- **Minimum health for breeding:** 75%

## Production

### Wool
Wool production is seasonal and only occurs during warm months.

| Season | Production |
|--------|------------|
| Spring | Active |
| Summer | Active |
| Fall | Reduced |
| Winter | None |

!!! note "Seasonal Production"
    Plan your shearing schedule for spring and summer to maximize wool harvest.

### Milk
Lactating ewes produce milk, though in smaller quantities than cattle.

### Manure Output

| Output Type | Amount |
|-------------|--------|
| Manure | Medium |
| Liquid Manure | Medium |

## Input Requirements

Sheep are grazers and require:

- Grass or hay
- Fresh water
- Mineral supplements (in mixed feed)

## Common Diseases

- [Mastitis](mastitis.md) - Affects lactating ewes
- [Foot and Mouth](foot_and_mouth.md) - Highly contagious

## Sheep Management Tips

!!! tip "Successful Sheep Farming"
    - **Seasonal planning** - Schedule shearing for warm months
    - **Predator protection** - Ensure secure fencing
    - **Foot care** - Regular hoof trimming prevents lameness
    - **Ram rotation** - Prevent inbreeding by rotating breeding rams
    - **Wool quality** - High-quality genetics improve wool value

## Economic Considerations

### Revenue Streams

| Product | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Wool | Seasonal, quality-dependent |
| Milk | From lactating ewes |
| Meat | Lamb and mutton |
| Breeding Stock | Premium genetics command high prices |

### Strengths
- Multiple products (wool, milk, meat)
- Lower feed requirements than cattle
- Good foraging ability
- Manageable size

### Challenges
- Seasonal wool production
- Susceptible to predation
- Foot health issues
- Weather sensitivity

## Visual Features

- Ear tags on both ears
- Breed-specific wool coloring
